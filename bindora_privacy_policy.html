Bindora Privacy Policy

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Bindora collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the Bindora mobile application. Bindora is designed as a local-first document organization app: your scanned documents, extracted document data, reminders, and library content are primarily processed and stored on your device.

Last updated: April 10, 2026

1. Who we are

Bindora is a mobile application published by Vaycen.

Contact email: vaycepro@gmail.com

2. What Bindora does

Bindora helps users scan paper documents, extract useful information locally, review or correct detected fields, organize documents into folders, create reminders, and retrieve documents later.

Bindora is currently a local-first app. At this stage, Bindora does not rely on its own backend server to store your documents in the cloud.

3. Information processed by the app

3.1 Information processed locally on your device

When you use Bindora, the app may process the following information locally on your device:

  • scanned document images;
  • PDF files generated from your scans;
  • OCR text extracted from documents;
  • detected document fields such as organization name, amount, date, time, IBAN, email address, or URL;
  • document titles, metadata, reminder details, folder assignment, and document status;
  • local notification data needed to remind you about a document or deadline.

This information is primarily stored and used locally on your device so that the app can function.

3.2 Information not intended to be sent to analytics services

Bindora is designed so that sensitive document content is not intended to be sent to Firebase Analytics, Crashlytics, or Performance Monitoring.

In particular, Bindora does not intend to send the following document content to analytics systems:

  • raw OCR text;
  • real document title;
  • names of people;
  • postal addresses;
  • email addresses extracted from documents;
  • phone numbers extracted from documents;
  • IBANs or bank account identifiers;
  • URLs extracted from documents;
  • exact amounts;
  • exact dates contained in documents;
  • customer references, file numbers, or similar identifiers.

4. Third-party services used by Bindora

Bindora may use the following third-party services depending on your device, platform, and app version:

  • Google Firebase Analytics for product analytics;
  • Firebase Crashlytics for crash reporting and error diagnostics;
  • Firebase Performance Monitoring for app performance measurement;
  • RevenueCat for purchase processing, entitlement management, and purchase restoration;
  • Google AdMob for advertising in the free version of the app.

5. Analytics and diagnostic data

Bindora may collect limited analytics, technical, and usage data to understand how the app is used, improve product quality, detect crashes, and measure performance.

Examples of data that may be collected through analytics or diagnostics include:

  • app version and build number;
  • device platform;
  • screen views and app navigation events;
  • high-level usage events such as scan started, scan completed, reminder created, or document opened;
  • technical status indicators such as result status, confidence buckets, count buckets, or duration metrics;
  • crash logs, non-fatal error reports, and technical diagnostics.

These analytics events are designed to avoid transmitting sensitive document content.

6. Advertising

Bindora may display ads through Google AdMob in the free version of the app.

Ads are intended to remain limited and should not appear on sensitive or critical screens such as document scanning, OCR processing, field validation, document details, or reminder creation flows.

Premium users may have ads removed depending on the purchased offer.

AdMob and its partners may process advertising-related data according to their own policies and applicable law. Where required, Bindora may request consent before serving personalized ads.

7. Purchases and premium access

Bindora may offer one-time purchases or other paid features managed through RevenueCat and the relevant app store billing system.

To provide premium access, restore purchases, and manage entitlements, purchase-related technical data may be processed by RevenueCat and the platform store provider.

Bindora does not directly process your full payment card details.

8. Legal bases for processing

Depending on your location and applicable law, Bindora may process information on the following legal bases:

  • to perform the service you request when using the app;
  • to pursue legitimate interests such as improving app reliability, security, and usability;
  • based on your consent, where consent is required, especially for certain analytics or advertising activities;
  • to comply with legal obligations.

9. Data storage and security

Bindora is designed to store documents and related metadata locally on your device. The app also includes security measures intended to protect locally stored data.

However, no software or device environment can guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for securing access to your device, including the use of device passcodes, biometric protection, and system updates where available.

10. Data sharing

Bindora does not sell your personal data.

Bindora may share limited data with third-party service providers only to the extent necessary to operate analytics, diagnostics, advertising, purchases, or legal compliance, including:

  • Google Firebase services;
  • Google AdMob;
  • RevenueCat;
  • Apple App Store and Google Play billing systems;
  • other service providers or authorities where required by law.

11. Data retention

Documents and reminders stored locally remain on your device until you delete them, uninstall the app, or remove the app data using your device settings.

Analytics, diagnostics, ad-related, or purchase-related data processed by third-party providers may be retained according to their own retention policies and legal obligations.

12. Your rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights regarding your personal data, including rights to access, delete, restrict, object, or request portability of certain data, as well as the right to withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

Because Bindora is primarily local-first, much of your document data remains directly under your control on your device. You can usually delete such data directly in the app or by deleting the app data from your device.
